Best Jollof Rice Recipe

Jollof rice is a spicy and savory West African dish that’s made differently from country to country. Nigerian jollof rice is prized for its delicious, smoky flavor and simplicity—to make it, you only need one pot! Follow along with us as we reveal how to whip up the best Nigerian jollof rice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 3 plum tomatoes
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup (237 ml) of chicken stock
  • 2 cups (400 g) of long-grain, parboiled rice
  • 2 cubes of chicken bouillon
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon (2.4 g) of white pepper
  • 2 white or red onions
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper
  • 2 tablespoons (30 ml) of tomato paste
  • ½ cup (118 ml) of vegetable oil
  • ½ teaspoon (0.5 g) of thyme

Cooking Steps:

  1. Wash the peppers, onion, and tomatoes.
  2. Blend the chopped ingredients together.
  3. Rinse the rice in water until it runs clear.
  4. Heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  5. Chop 1 onion and saute it in the oil for 4 minutes.
  6. Cook the tomato paste for 5 minutes.
  7. Add in the pepper puree and spices.
  8. Add the rice, chicken stock, water, and salt.
  9. Cover and cook the rice for 20 min.
  10. Simmer the rice for an additional 10-20 min.
  11. Fluff and serve the jollof rice.
  12. Store the rice in the fridge for 5 days.
